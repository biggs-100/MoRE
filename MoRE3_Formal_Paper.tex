\begin{abstract}
\noindent 
Static architectures in deep learning fail to adapt to non-stationary environments without catastrophic forgetting or structural saturation. We introduce \textbf{MoRE-3}, a sovereign neuro-symbolic architecture that addresses these limitations. MoRE-3 utilizes the \textbf{R-Perceptron} as its fundamental unit, featuring an intrinsic novelty gate $g$ that detects familiarity through cosine resonance. To enable massive scaling, we implement a hybrid vector engine utilizing \textbf{FAISS-IVF}, achieving $O(\log M)$ search complexity. Furthermore, we propose a biologically inspired \textbf{Mitosis} mechanism that triggers autonomous structural growth based on information entropy and familiarity depletion. Validated on real-text benchmarks, MoRE-3 demonstrates $100\%$ accuracy on known classes and surgical structural expansion (3-to-4 experts) upon encountering novel semantic domains.
\end{abstract}

% ===== 1. INTRODUCTION =====
\section{Introduction}
Current LLM paradigms rely on fixed-capacity transformers and gradient-based updates, which are susceptible to hallucinations and the inability to distinguish known from unknown data. The human brain, conversely, utilizes associative memory and structural plasticity to grow as it learns.

We propose \textbf{MoRE-3}, a hierarchical system of Resonance-Experts. Our contributions are: (1) A formal framework for resonant routing; (2) A scalable hybrid inference engine utilizing FAISS; and (3) An autonomous mitosis mechanism that allows the network to grow without human intervention.

% ===== 2. THE RESONANT ENGINE =====
\section{The Resonant Engine}
The core of MoRE-3 is the R-Perceptron, parameterized by $\Theta = (\mathbf{K}, \mathbf{V}, \mathbf{s}, \theta, \tau, \beta, k, \gamma)$.

\subsection{Hybrid Scalable Search}
For large-scale associative memory, linear search $O(M)$ is prohibitive. We define a transition function $\Phi(M)$:
\begin{equation}
\text{SearchMode} = \begin{cases} \text{PyTorch (Dense)} & \text{if } M < 1024 \\ \text{FAISS (IVF-Flat)} & \text{if } M \ge 1024 \end{cases}
\end{equation}
This hybrid approach preserves the fidelity of the novelty gate while providing sub-millisecond latency for millions of prototypes.

\subsection{Novelty Gating and Familiarity}
The familiarity $f$ is calculated as the maximum cosine resonance. The novelty gate $g$ provides the "surprise" signal:
\begin{equation}
g(\mathbf{x}) = 1 - \sigma(\beta \cdot (f(\mathbf{x}) - \theta))
\end{equation}
If $g > 0.5$, the input is gated as novelty, protecting the expert from noisy updates and signaling the need for growth.

\section{Autonomous Structural Evolution}
MoRE-3 monitors the "health" of each expert via two signals: Assignment Entropy ($H$) and Mean Familiarity ($\bar{f}$).

\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\REQUIRE Experience Buffer $\mathcal{B}$, Thresholds $\theta_h, \theta_f$
\STATE $\bar{f} \gets \text{mean}(f \in \mathcal{B})$
